Working From Home

 

Our tips to weather the crisis without getting eye strain

The top of the screen should be at about the level of your eyes, keyboard at elbow height and feet flat on the floor or on a footrest. The screen should be at least 70cm from your face to avoid eye strain. When you stare at the glare of a computer screen for too long, you are making your eyes work harder. This habit can lead to digital eye strain that is characterized by headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and neck and shoulder pain.

Eye strain could be common for those who don’t take breaks from the computer screen. To give your eyes a break, follow the 20-20-20 rule: “Take a 20-second break to view something 20 metres away every 20 minutes.”

Also, remember to blink, it is something that you do automatically but when concentrating hard, that blinking rate gets reduced which leads to drying of eyes. Sometimes it helps just closing your eyes briefly for a few seconds.

Try to focus on relaxing. If you concentrate on the screen your muscles start to tense up that means your shoulders pulls up neck muscles starts straining which may lead to an aching body at the end of the day.

If possible, work near a window with natural light that is preferably at a 90-degree angle to your screen, as opposed to right behind you. The natural light is better for you than artificial light. A window with a view also helps your eye muscles relax.
